welcome to tokyo, japan!

Japan is an archipelago in East Asia. The kanji characters that represent Japan mean "sun origin" and it is also called "Land of the Rising Sun". There are over five thousand islands that comprise this country in the Pacific Ocean. The nation is divided into 47 prefectures in eight regions. The population is approximately 126 million. Japan has the third largest economy by GDP alone. The government of Japan is a unitary constitutional monarchy with an Emperor at the header and supported by an elected legislature, the National Diet. In the past century, Japan has grown to become a worldwide power, boosted by the success of its economy and rapidly developing tourism and science and technology sectors.

japanese customs

The Japanese hold themselves to a strong sense of etiquette that dictates social order and behaviors. The bow is a form of showing respect, remorse, or used as a general greeting. Various degrees of bowing correspond to various displays of respect. Taking off footwear is another customary thing to do in Japan. It is especially important to do so when entering one’s private homes. Most customs are rooted in the idea of respect and showing deference to higher authority.

travel tips

  1. walking and eating is not polite and is considered bad manners
  2. download google translate app to help with the language barrier
  3. get suica cards to travel on public transporation
  4. don’t use cell phones on trains and buses
  5. drive and walk on the left side
  6. carry tissues or hand towels to maintain hygiene
  7. it is not customary to tip; tipping is considered rude
  8. public transportation is available only until midnight
  9. form an orderly line while waiting for public transportation
  10. take photos only in areas where they’re permitted; there will usually be signs to indicate where photos are appropriate
  11. slurping is a sign of enjoying food and do not be afraid to slurp noodles especially
  12. under no circumstances should rice be doused in soy sauce, doing so is an offense to the chef’s ability to season food
  13. do not stick chopsticks upright in a bowl
  14. in a traditional japanese restaurant, take shoes off when walking in
  15. remember to bow as a sign of respect
