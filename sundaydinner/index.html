sunday dinner

november 22, 2015 7:36PM

The weekend prior to Thanksgiving was the perfect time to workshop some of our favorite family dishes. My dad and I prepared a quiet sunday dinner together: stir fried eggs and green beans, an aromatic dish of leafy greens, and a lotus root and fish patty that accompanies rice perfectly. As delicious as the meal was, spending an evening with my dad in the kitchen was even better.

My favorite dish of the night was the lotus root and fish patty, a recipe my dad experimented with one Sunday night a few years ago and has remained a staple in our family menu ever since. The process involves peeling and finely dicing the lotus root and then combining it with the fish to form a solid patty. The patty is fried to a golden brown color, giving it a crisp exterior and warm interior. The leafy greens are cooked in a hot wok with a little oil and garlic. The eggs and green beans are my speciality: they only require occasional stiring and seasoning. When it comes to cooking, my dad is the chef and I am the secondary cook. I'm terribly clumsy in the kitchen, but based on the tasty food that goes out on the dinner table, no one will ever know.
